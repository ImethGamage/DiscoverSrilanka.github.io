Best places to Travel in Sri Lanka

Sigiriya

Sigiriya is an ancient rock fortress located in the northern Matale District near the town of Dambulla in the Central Province, Sri Lanka. The name refers to a site of historical and archaeological significance that is dominated by a massive column of rock nearly 200 metres high.

View More

Sinharaja Rain Forest

Sinharaja Forest Reserve is a forest reserve and a biodiversity hotspot in Sri Lanka. And this is a world heritage. The unique relation of flora and fauna in this forest makes it one of the hotspots in the world.

View More

Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa)

The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic or Sri Dalada Maligawa is a Buddhist temple in Kandy, Sri Lanka. It is located in the royal palace complex of the former Kingdom of Kandy, which houses the relic of the tooth of the Buddha.

View More

Ancient City of Polonnaruwa

The Ancient City of Polonnaruwa was the second capital of Sri Lanka for three centuries between the 11th to 13th century after the destruction of Anuradhapura Kingdom in 993. It is located in north central province of Sri Lanka. There are so many monuments in Polonnaruwa, so its grate place to Visit.

View More

Ella

Ella is a small town in the Badulla District of Uva Province, Sri Lanka. It is located at an elevation of 1,041 m (3,427 ft) above sea level in the Ella Gap, a gap in the mountains of the island's central highlands.

View More

Adam’s Peak (Sri Padaya)

Adam’s Peak is a 2,243 m (7,359 ft) tall conical sacred mountain located in central Sri Lanka. It is a popular pilgrimage site for Sri Lankans and tourists alike. The typical climb is started in the middle of the night so you can reach the peak for a breathtaking Adams peak sunrise

View More

Mirissa beach

If you're looking for a tropical beach experience, head to Mirissa. It is very beautiful. It's a popular spot for whale watching, and you can also relax on the golden sandy beaches and enjoy water sports.

View More

yala national park

Yala National Park is the most visited and second largest national park in Sri Lanka. It borders the Indian Ocean and consists of five blocks, two of which are now open to the public. The park is very rich in wildlife and you’re virtually certain to encounter elephants, crocodiles, buffaloes and monkeys.
